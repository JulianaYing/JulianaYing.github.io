The South China Tiger,
a critically endangered subspecies native to southern China.
With striking stripes and an imposing stance,
these majestic creatures have sadly become "functionally extinct" in the wild due to relentless poaching and habitat loss. Conservation efforts now hinge on captive breeding, fostering hope for their eventual return to the wild.
